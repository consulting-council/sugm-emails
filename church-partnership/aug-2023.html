CHURCH SPOTLIGHT

 

Since its founding in March 2005, New Walk Christian Church has been a pillar of truth and a life-giving branch to a diverse group of marginalized people in the city of Burien. Never one to deny help to people in need, New Walk does whatever it can to ensure our homeless neighbors are fed, both physically and spiritually, clothed, and supported in other ways.

 

In partnership with other ministries and organizations, New Walk provides counseling and connections to services necessary to help our hurting neighbors experience healing and hope

 

Through its Feed the Need Pantry, New Walk provides opportunities for our neighbors in need to come and select groceries every Wednesday from 9am – 1pm. Working alongside other organizations, New Walk also supports smaller churches and ministries to fill the food insecurity needs in the neighborhoods they serve.

 

Additionally, New Walk conducts a weekly discipleship class for a faith-based, in-house recovery program, and a weekly Bible study in an assisted living facility for elderly people. These are just a few of the God-directed ministries the church runs to serve our marginalized neighbors in and around the city of Burien.

 

New Walk’s Senior Pastor, Dr. Walter Heyman, Jr., states, “Without the support and encouragement of Seattle’s Union Gospel Mission, there is no way we could help so many of our marginalized neighbors, not only fill their stomachs, and put clothes on their backs, but most importantly, find Jesus Christ. And for that, we are most grateful.”

 

If you would like to learn more about how your church can meet the needs of our homeless neighbors in your community, please get in touch with your Mission Church Representative, or email us at church@ugm.org .
