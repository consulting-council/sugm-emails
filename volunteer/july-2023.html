MISSION SPOTLIGHT
A YEAR OF FAITHFUL SERVICE
Robert and Louetta Koehn came to the Mission last summer because they were seeking a place to minister and serve over the span of a year. They tried out volunteering with an assortment of organizations, but, as Robert shared,
“I just kept getting drawn back to the Mission and all that you are doing here!”
The Koehns have now volunteered over 300 hours with the Mission - serving meals, going out on Search + Rescue, mentoring our guests, and even participating in our Volunteer Advisory Committee. It’s seldom that one couple has blessed so many of our neighbors so much in such a short span of time.
As Robert and Louetta finish up their year of ministry here in Seattle , we send them out with a blessing and prayer that God will continue leading them in their life of service.

NOURISH LIVES AND BUILD RELATIONSHIPS!
Imagine the life-changing power of a simple act like sharing a meal. You can be a part of that change through the Mission’s Bring-A-Meal (BAM) program. Together, we can combat hunger, build relationships and stronger communities, and foster team spirit.
The BAM program not only offers an opportunity to serve our homeless neighbors, but also serves as an incredible team-building experience. Engage your colleagues, friends, or family members in this meaningful endeavor. Working together to prepare and serve meals creates a shared sense of purpose and deepens bonds while making a positive impact on our homeless neighbors.
Let's all harness the collective goodwill of our community. Join the BAM program today, and experience the joy of nourishing lives, strengthening communities, and building stronger connections within your team, friends, or family.
