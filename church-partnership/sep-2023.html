MISSION SNAPSHOT

CHURCH SPOTLIGHT

It only takes sharing one meal to break the ice and begin a relationship. Faith Church is a neighbor to KentHOPE, which houses our stabilization program for women and children. Hearing of the need for churches to partner with the Mission in providing food and relationships, several women from the church teamed up to prepare one meal per month for KentHOPE guests. They soon discovered that God was at work in the hearts of the women coming to KentHOPE. The shared meals broke the ice and led to conversations and relationships. For several years, these women and their small team faithfully gathered ingredients and prepared a meal for KentHOPE every month!

 

Then, the needs changed. KentHOPE staff recognized they needed support with more meals. They also realized they needed meals that could be prepared quickly when enrollment surged or when a meal didn’t arrive as planned. The women at Faith Church learned of the new needs, and stepped up to the plate (pun intended) again.

 

plate (pun intended) again. The increased needs meant more people needed to be involved. The Faith Church team cast the vision for the church family to partner. They began providing ingredients for a specific menu every month, and invited more church members to participate on cooking nights. The Faith family responded, and the Mission has been blessed by their efforts for many years since. Faith Church now delivers more than 500 nutritious and delicious meals for breakfast, lunch, and dinner each month. These meals are frozen and stored at the church, and then delivered to KentHOPE freezers as needed.

 

This ministry helps fulfill Faith’s core value of being an outwardly focused church by bringing a gospel response to the needs of the Kent community. In addition to meeting urgent needs of the women and children at KentHOPE, the meal prep acts as an entry point for relationship and increased volunteerism by the Faith Church family throughout the Mission.

 

The Mission is grateful for the meals provided and for the relationships formed by Faith Church’s partnership with Kent HOPE!

WAYS TO ENGAGE

  • Our Search + Rescue Search + Rescue teams go out, day and night, to search out and find men, women, and children who are homeless, hungry, and hurting.   • Would you like a resource to help equip your leadership team to better love and serve our homeless neighbors? In addition to our Engaging Homelessness trainings, we recommend the book The Church: Helping or Hurting , by Michael Dye . If you are interested in discussing this book, please get in touch with your Mission Church Representative.
